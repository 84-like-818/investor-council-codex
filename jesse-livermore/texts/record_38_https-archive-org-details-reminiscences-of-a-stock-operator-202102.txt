Reminiscences of a Stock Operator is a 1923 roman à clef by American author Edwin Lefèvre. It is told in the first person by a character inspired by the life of stock trader Jesse Livermore up to that point.
The American stock market is pretty crazy at times. A recent example is the Gamestop bubble. The market has always been interesting and somewhat maddening to try to figure out.
This book was written almost one hundred years ago and still has some good advice. Congress has since passed many laws to curb trading practices but when the same people are passing laws and also can profit from them, corruption tends to ensue.
