## Working Distillation

Jesse Livermore is anchored here as an American stock trader and financier whose public image is built from three overlapping layers: direct trading-method texts attributed to him, later biographies and market books about him, and the much more influential but partly literary proxy portrait in Reminiscences of a Stock Operator.

The most important research caution is that the Livermore legend is mediated. The cleanest public full text currently in hand is Reminiscences, which is indispensable for understanding how he was mythologized, but it should not be treated as a verbatim autobiography. To separate fact from legend, the next validation layer has to come from newspapers, court or estate records, and higher-quality biographies.

The corpus already supports a workable structure for distillation: identity and dates are stable, core market-method texts and the main narrative proxy are present, and several later biographies are identified even when only page-level captures or metadata are available.
