# Biographical Episode Index 1893-1940

This file gives the product a chronological map of the Livermore life and legend. It is not here to make every episode equally certain. It is here to separate what the mentor can lean on confidently from what should stay qualified.

## 1893-1897: Board-boy and bucket-shop apprenticeship

- Why it matters: this is the origin of the tape-reading frame, number memory, and pattern recognition habit.
- Best source route: Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences`, opening pages.
- Product use: use this period when explaining why Livermore starts from price action rather than thesis writing.

## Early New York years: first big mismatch between skill and market structure

- Why it matters: the bucket-shop edge did not transfer cleanly to the real market; losses became part of the method.
- Best source route: `Reminiscences`, early New York reverses and `never argue with the tape`.
- Product use: use this when the user has a setup that works in one regime but not in another.

## 1907 panic reputation

- Why it matters: this is a major anchor of the Livermore legend as a bear operator in a panic.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Fourth Down` (retrospective, 1934-03-19).
- Product use: use carefully and label it as retrospective media framing rather than direct self-description.

## 1915 bankruptcy and reset

- Why it matters: repeated financial destruction is part of the Livermore pattern and explains why discipline matters more than brilliance.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Fourth Down`.
- Product use: use when explaining that insight without process control is not enough.

## 1924 public market warning

- Why it matters: shows Livermore in the public eye as a market operator whose doubts could move sentiment.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Livermore's Doubts` (1924-02-25).
- Product use: use when the user asks about public bearishness, tape signals, or market influence.

## 1925 wheat episode

- Why it matters: this is a clean case for public suspicion, crowded bearishness, and the line between timing skill and manipulation narrative.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Wheat` and `No Proof`.
- Product use: use when discussing crowded shorts, public blame, and how market operators get narrated in a panic.

## 1927 operator fame

- Why it matters: the later `Boy Plunger` retrospective ties 1927 into the public mythology of Livermore as a large speculative operator.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Boy Plunger` (1940-12-09).
- Product use: use as a public-image anchor, not as precise doctrine.

## 1929 crash reputation

- Why it matters: this is the episode most users already associate with Livermore.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Bankers v. Panic` and `Boy Plunger`.
- Product use: use for crisis psychology, short-side reputation, and the danger of treating one famous win as a complete doctrine.

## 1934 fourth failure

- Why it matters: this makes the `genius but repeated collapse` pattern impossible to ignore.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Fourth Down`.
- Product use: use when the user romanticizes bold calls without process discipline.

## 1940 death and retrospective framing

- Why it matters: the death crystallized the public retrospective on Livermore's brilliance, instability, and tragedy.
- Best source route: `TIME` `Boy Plunger`, plus the stable identity anchors.
- Product use: use when explaining why the mentor should sound disciplined and unsentimental, not romantic.
