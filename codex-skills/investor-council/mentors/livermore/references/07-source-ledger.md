# Source Ledger

This ledger answers one question: what public materials, archival anchors, and derived research packs does the Livermore mentor actually stand on today?

Unlike Buffett, Livermore does not leave behind a clean yearly shareholder-letter archive. This pack therefore has to be explicit about what is direct, what is proxy, and what is contemporary media.

## Identity And Public Anchors

1. Wikidata entity `Q724153`
   https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q724153
   Role: stabilizes identity, birth and death dates, occupation, and canonical name.

2. Wikimedia Commons category for Jesse Livermore
   https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Jesse_Livermore
   Role: stabilizes public identity, image leads, and date anchors for the product.

## Primary Proxy Text

1. Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences of a Stock Operator`
   https://www.gutenberg.org/files/60979/60979-h/60979-h.htm
   Role: this is the cleanest public full text in hand. It is not a verbatim autobiography, but it is the indispensable proxy text for Livermore-style tape reading, trend following, patience, losses, and self-reliance.

2. Open Library work page for `Reminiscences of a stock operator`
   https://openlibrary.org/works/OL3472215W
   Role: anchors the work family around the proxy narrative.

3. Internet Archive detail page for `reminiscencesofs0000lefe_l7g7`
   https://archive.org/details/reminiscencesofs0000lefe_l7g7
   Role: anchors the archive trail and modern commentary editions of the proxy narrative.

## Contemporary Media And Historical Scaffolding

1. `TIME`: `Livermore's Doubts` (1924-02-25)
   https://time.com/archive/6663496/business-livermores-doubts/
   Role: shows his public market influence and the distinction between technical market timing and broader macro commentary.

2. `TIME`: `Wheat` (1925-03-23)
   https://time.com/archive/6818758/business-finance-wheat-7/
   Role: anchors the 1925 grain episode and public perception of Livermore as a bear raider.

3. `TIME`: `No Proof` (1925-05-25)
   https://time.com/archive/6860980/national-affairs-no-proof/
   Role: extends the 1925 wheat story and shows how quickly market reputation turns into manipulation suspicion.

4. `TIME`: `Bankers v. Panic` (1929-11-04)
   https://time.com/archive/6778424/business-finance-bankers-v-panic/
   Role: anchors his reputation during the 1929 crash and the public narrative around the bear campaign.

5. `TIME`: `Fourth Down` (1934-03-19)
   https://time.com/archive/6894439/business-fourth-down/
   Role: anchors the 1907 story in retrospective form, the 1915 bankruptcy, and the 1934 fourth failure.

6. `TIME`: `Boy Plunger` (1940-12-09)
   https://time.com/archive/6764404/business-boy-plunger/
   Role: anchors the late-life decline, 1927 and 1929 retrospective public image, and the 1940 death context.
