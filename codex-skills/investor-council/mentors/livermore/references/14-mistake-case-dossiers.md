# Mistake Case Dossiers

This file exists so the Livermore mentor can talk about failure like Livermore's record demands: not as a morality play, and not as a glamour story, but as repeated evidence that brilliance without process discipline breaks.

## Bucket-shop skill carried too far into a different market
- What happened: early pattern-reading success in bucket shops did not transfer cleanly to New York execution and holding conditions.
- What he got right at the time: he really did have unusual number memory and pattern sensitivity.
- What he actually got wrong: he treated an edge from one environment as if it were the whole game.
- How to defend against it now: separate signal quality from market structure, liquidity, execution, and holding horizon.
- First-hand / best source anchors:
  - Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences`, opening chapters.
  - `The game taught me the game` anchor in `15-paragraph-source-anchors.md`.

## Tips and borrowed conviction
- What happened: the proxy text is explicit that tips create dependency and bad exits.
- What he got right at the time: market gossip can look actionable in fast tape conditions.
- What he actually got wrong: outside conviction cannot substitute for a self-owned thesis and exit plan.
- How to defend against it now: if you cannot state your own trigger and invalidation, you do not own the position.
- First-hand / best source anchors:
  - Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences` `A man must believe in himself`.

## Constant action and style drift
- What happened: repeated damage came from needing to act, not from lacking intelligence.
- What he got right at the time: being alert and active matters in speculation.
- What he actually got wrong: action bias erodes edge when the market is not paying for activity.
- How to defend against it now: define the setup first; if no setup exists, waiting is the trade.
- First-hand / best source anchors:
  - Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences` `desire for constant action`.
  - `Nobody can catch all the fluctuations`.

## Not sitting with the right position
- What happened: the proxy text makes clear that being right too early or jumping in and out destroyed the real money.
- What he got right at the time: his directional read could be right.
- What he actually got wrong: he under-monetized the main move by over-managing it.
- How to defend against it now: patience belongs to a confirmed position; once confirmed, stop trading every wiggle.
- First-hand / best source anchors:
  - Project Gutenberg `Reminiscences` `It was always my sitting`.
  - `The market does not beat them. They beat themselves`.

## Life-level leverage and repeated collapse
- What happened: public history records repeated bankruptcies and a tragic final decline despite periods of brilliance and fame.
- What he got right at the time: he could still read tape and dominate certain episodes.
- What he actually got wrong: spectacular trading insight did not reliably become durable personal financial resilience.
- How to defend against it now: protect capital structure, lifestyle leverage, and psychological durability, not just the next trade.
- First-hand / best source anchors:
  - `TIME` `Fourth Down`: 1934 fourth failure and earlier collapses.
  - `TIME` `Boy Plunger`: late-life retrospective after death.
