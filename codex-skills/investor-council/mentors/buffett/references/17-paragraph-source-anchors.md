# Paragraph Source Anchors

This file is for the moment when the product needs to sound less like a generic value-investing explainer and more like a research assistant with a paper trail.
Each topic gives the best paragraph-level or phrase-level anchor to revisit in the official material or the meeting transcript layer.

## Buyback discipline
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/2011ar/2011ar.pdf`
- Anchor cue: repurchase-policy discussion around buying Berkshire only below a set multiple of book value, with excess liquidity preserved.
- Why it matters: this is the cleanest early formal statement of when buybacks add value instead of destroy it.

## Book value losing relevance
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/2018ar/2018ar.pdf`
- Anchor cue: search for the phrase `lost the relevance it once had`.
- Why it matters: this is the bridge from old book-value shorthand to the more direct intrinsic-value frame.

## Apple and owner economics
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/2018ar/2018ar.pdf`
- Anchor cue: the look-through earnings discussion plus the Apple holding context.
- Why it matters: it shows why Buffett can treat Apple as a business with owner economics rather than as a hot technology trade.

## The simple arithmetic of a buyback
- Best source: `https://buffett.cnbc.com/video/2019/05/06/morning-session---2019-berkshire-hathaway-annual-meeting.html`
- Anchor cue: sync transcript around the line `So, it's very simple arithmetic`.
- Why it matters: this is the best short-form meeting-language anchor for Berkshire buybacks and intrinsic value.

## Tesco and dawdling
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/2014ar/2014ar.pdf`
- Anchor cue: search for `leisurely pace in making sales proved costly` and `dawdling`.
- Why it matters: this is a paragraph-level admission of slow error correction, not just a generic statement that mistakes happen.

## Dexter as the worst deal
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2007ltr.pdf`
- Anchor cue: search for `worst deal I've made`.
- Why it matters: this is the sharpest direct anchor for overestimating moat durability and misusing Berkshire stock as purchase currency.

## Precision Castparts overpayment
- Best source: `https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/2021ar/2021ar.pdf`
- Anchor cue: search for `paid too much for the company`.
- Why it matters: this is the cleanest official annual-report admission that a good asset can still be a bad investment because of price paid.

## Airline exit and structural error
- Best source: `https://buffett.cnbc.com/video/2020/05/20/berkshire-hathaway-annual-meeting-2020---part-1.html`
- Anchor cue: sync transcript around the airline-industry discussion and why Berkshire sold the full airline position set.
- Why it matters: this is the strongest meeting-language anchor for changing the thesis when industry economics deteriorate.

## Temporary shock vs permanent impairment
- Best source: `https://buffett.cnbc.com/video/2020/05/20/berkshire-hathaway-annual-meeting-2020---part-1.html`
- Anchor cue: transcript around the March 23 Fed-action discussion.
- Why it matters: this gives the clearest real-time Buffett distinction between frozen markets, emergency policy response, and long-term business damage.

## Governance is not cosmetic
- Best source: `https://buffett.cnbc.com/2014-berkshire-hathaway-annual-meeting/`
- Anchor cue: Coca-Cola executive-pay discussion in the 2014 meeting materials.
- Why it matters: this is where the mentor can show that governance questions are really capital-allocation and trust questions.
