# Investment Case Maps

This file maps Buffett's major investments or transactions back to doctrine.
It is not a biography. It is a product-facing answer to one question: what did this case teach him?

## GEICO: low cost model + float + operating efficiency
- Core idea: GEICO is a base case for understanding a great insurance business.
- Doctrine mapping: cost advantage, underwriting discipline, and float only matter when underwriting stays sound.
- Product use: answer why some capital-heavy financial businesses can still be excellent businesses.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, GEICO.

## American Express: trust franchise + payment network + recovery after stress
- Core idea: this is not only a cheap-valuation story; it is a high-grade franchise story.
- Doctrine mapping: a great business can be hurt by a bad event without losing the core advantage.
- Product use: answer temporary shock vs permanent impairment.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, American Express.

## Coca-Cola: brand + distribution + pricing power
- Core idea: this is the classic global consumer-moat template.
- Doctrine mapping: strong brand, repeat purchase, light asset intensity, and pricing power can compound for decades.
- Product use: explain what a moat looks like when it truly prints cash.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, Coca-Cola.

## ISCAR: people + business + cultural fit
- Core idea: the 2006 material shows what Buffett values when buying an entire business.
- Doctrine mapping: trusted managers, culture, and products that help customers earn more money make a powerful combination.
- Product use: answer what a great company plus a great management team looks like.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, ISCAR.

## BNSF: capital-heavy but essential infrastructure
- Core idea: Buffett does not only buy light-asset businesses.
- Doctrine mapping: if the business is essential, demand is durable, and returns are understandable, capital intensity does not disqualify it.
- Product use: answer how to think about rail, utility, grid, and network infrastructure assets.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, BNSF.

## Heinz: great brand + deal design
- Core idea: Buffett does not only evaluate the company; he also evaluates the structure of the deal.
- Doctrine mapping: a good asset can be turned into a better risk/reward by using common, preferred, and warrants together.
- Product use: let the mentor talk about deal design, not only PE multiples.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, Heinz.

## Apple: concentrated ownership after real understanding
- Core idea: this is not "Buffett suddenly became a tech investor"; it is the result of understanding ecosystem, loyalty, and capital return.
- Doctrine mapping: circle of competence is not an industry label; it is whether you truly understand the business.
- Product use: answer why Buffett can own a business that looks like a tech company without betraying his framework.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, Apple.

## Kraft Heinz / Precision Castparts: a serious mentor must also carry an error map
- Core idea: a believable Buffett mentor must contain mistakes, not just success stories.
- Doctrine mapping: paying too much, misreading long-run economics, or overrating operational fixability can turn a good business into a bad investment.
- Product use: let the mentor say "the thesis broke" or "the price was wrong" without hedging.
- Source anchors: see `14-case-source-anchors.md`, Kraft Heinz / Precision Castparts, then continue into `15-mistake-case-dossiers.md`.
